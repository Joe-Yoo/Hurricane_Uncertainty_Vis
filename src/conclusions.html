Thank You for Testing!

We appreciate you taking the time to explore our visualization

We hope our design helps you understand the spatial uncertainty in hurricane track forecasts — showing how ensemble models reveal a wide cone of possible paths, and how even small differences in initial atmospheric conditions can lead to substantially different landfall locations within 48–72 hours of forecast.

We also hope the linked views make it easier to see how tracks that appear spatially similar can differ significantly in predicted wind speed, temperature, and precipitation — illustrating why relying on a single "best track" forecast can underestimate the range of hazardous conditions a location may face.

Finally, we hope the location-based filtering gives you a more targeted picture of the risk posed to a specific community, complementing traditional cone-of-uncertainty maps. The two interaction modes — self-select and within-radius — are designed to support both exploratory and task-focused analysis styles.
