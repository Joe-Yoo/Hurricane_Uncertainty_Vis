Conclusions

What we learned from exploring hurricane track uncertainty

Spatial Uncertainty

Ensemble track models reveal a wide cone of possible hurricane paths. Even small differences in initial atmospheric conditions can lead to substantially different landfall locations within 48–72 hours of forecast.

Attribute Variation Across Tracks

Tracks that appear spatially similar can differ significantly in predicted wind speed, temperature, and precipitation. Relying on a single "best track" forecast can therefore underestimate the range of hazardous conditions a location may face.

Location-Based Filtering

Filtering ensemble members by proximity to a user-defined location provides a more targeted picture of the risk posed to a specific community — a useful complement to traditional cone-of-uncertainty maps.

Design Takeaways

Linked views — spatial on the left, attribute-encoded on the right — help users connect geographic patterns with meteorological meaning. Interaction modes (self-select vs. within-radius) accommodate both exploratory and task-focused analysis styles.
